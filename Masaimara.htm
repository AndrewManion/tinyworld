The Mara

I have to admit that I like birthdays. In a strange way. So, the day before the seventeenth, I left the farm early in the morning on the back of Paul's motorcycle. After a long road and some bus changes, I arrived at the Talek River and asked every land cruiser around if they had a free spot in a safari tomorrow. It wasn't until late in the night that I found a hitchike through the Talek Gate. We entered just before sunrise and everything was still but the hot air baloons. There were zebras grazing as soft as sunrise. There were lions that sunbathed, a cheetah which posed, elephants out on maneuvers and the savage wildabeasts which stampeeded through rivers in dramatic fashion. Twenty-seven felt like a miniscule number inside all this vastness.
