New Year in Old Britain.

After a short visit home for Christmas I was off running for London. I said my final goodbyes in New York and woke up across the pond. There, the Hokies were out in numbers. We drank Guiness, rode busses with Shelbys, and disco'd into the new year. Days later we left the city behind us and drove out towards Wales. At the climax of our road adventure the snow left us stranded on ancient grounds. We obliged the inn keeper to finish the barrel of mead and then took shelter in a cold tent not quite big enough for three.
