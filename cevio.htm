Horse Riding in Stone Valleys.

When Patrizia picked me up in Locarno I was awe-struck by Spring. The lake was cool blue and the sun was shining down. We chose to stay and walk through the city because shade had already fallen over Patrizia's home in Cevio. The next afternoon I was on the back of Matisse, the new boy in the stable, and we galloped between stone walls first built by Romans. I looked after the horses and the donkeys and sculpted a new entry to the stone home. This and other surrounding villages were abandoned centuries ago as the difficulties of farming in the mountains had sent the people on boats to New York. Now I was there to learn how to chisel new faces into the rocks which were everywhere.
